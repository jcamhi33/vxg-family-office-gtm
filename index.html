First PE Wins

01
Jacob + David
Run the Main Street Capital webinar — MiM's first PE-native live event
This is the single most important milestone in the entire motion. It is live proof that the format works, the messaging lands, and the PE audience shows up. Every response signal — questions asked, attendee seniority, follow-up requests, opt-ins after — must be captured and analyzed before Phase 3 begins.
Target attendance: 15–30 PE-adjacent attendees. Success is not just head count — it is quality of engagement. Two senior PE principals asking smart questions is worth more than 50 passive viewers. Post-event: hot lead list within 48 hours, follow-up sequence live within 72.

02
All
Execute multi-threaded outreach — hit PE principals and portco CEOs simultaneously
This is the implied virality play. When the PE principal sees MiM content at the same time a portco CEO sees it independently, both feel like the introduction is warm and inevitable — not a coordinated pitch. The conversion probability increases dramatically at both levels when the two threads are running in parallel.
Ratio rule: For every PE firm in outreach, identify and reach out to 3–5 of their portfolio companies independently. Jacob leads PE principal thread. David leads portco CEO thread. Messages must feel personal at every level — never templated.
🏢
03
David
Convert first 1–3 PE-sourced clients to paid engagements
The most likely entry point is Quick Start™ at $9,500–$15,000 — the lowest-friction program with the clearest deliverable. Target portcos with a clear MiM-ICP fit: relationship-driven B2B companies in manufacturing, distribution, or construction verticals that are PE-backed and have a growth mandate from their principal. David owns the close.
Qualification signal: Does the PE principal have a value creation mandate? Does the portco CEO acknowledge growth is stalled or founder-dependent? Does the company pass the relationship-driven business test? Three yes answers = high-priority close. One qualified Quick Start™ pays for the cost of the entire Phase 1 effort.
♻️
04
Eric
Repurpose the Main Street webinar into a full content stack
Every live webinar is a content factory. The Main Street session must produce: one long-form blog post anchored to PE-relevant keywords, three to five LinkedIn short clips from the best insight moments, a nurture email sequence for all attendees who didn't immediately convert, and a one-page leave-behind for hot leads. This content stack keeps the PE audience warm between live events and starts building the organic PE content engine.
SEO anchor keywords: "private equity portfolio growth," "PE operating system," "value creation B2B company," "AI for portfolio companies." These map directly to the /insights/private-equity/ content cluster in the website architecture already planned.

Every PE and family office contact in the system gets tagged, tiered, and visible in a single pipeline view. No more intelligence sitting in email inboxes or spreadsheets. The RDOS PE pipeline becomes the source of truth for who we are in conversations with, where they are in the funnel, and what the next action is for every contact.

06
David / Jacob
Validate PE-linked client numbers and map all clients to PE/FO owners
Before Phase 3 scaling decisions are made, the team needs accurate numbers. How many current MiM clients are PE-backed or family-office-owned? Who are their principals? Are those principals warm to MiM? The answer to those three questions determines Phase 3 targeting strategy. This validation was flagged as a key decision from the May 20 session and must be complete before July 4.
Why this matters: One existing client whose PE principal becomes a distribution partner could immediately unlock 3–5 additional portco conversations — at zero acquisition cost. This is likely the fastest single path to the first closed deal.
Family office channel — activating now
Family office → portco introductions
Family offices hold board-level authority over their portfolio companies. A single FO relationship is worth a direct introduction to 3–8 portfolio companies in a single conversation. HCPI, Valador, Borgman, and ACVI are already in the network. Begin activating introductions in Phase 2.
Warm vs. cold outreach ratio
One-third of all PE/FO outreach comes from warm existing relationships. Two-thirds is net-new targeted contact. This ratio prevents over-reliance on any single relationship and starts building the cold outreach muscle for Phase 3 and 4 at scale.
No paid spend in Phase 2
The team aligned clearly on May 20: no paid spend until at least one webinar round is complete and learnings are captured. Phase 2 is organic-only. Paid activation is a Phase 4 lever, enabled by validated CAC data from Phase 3.
Risks to manage in Phase 2
High
Webinar attendance is low or wrong audience
If the first event draws operations managers instead of PE principals, the learning is skewed. Pre-qualify every invitee. Jerry's direct network should guarantee seniority in the room.
High
Multi-thread outreach feels coordinated
If a PE principal and their portco CEO compare notes and realize MiM pitched both at the same time with similar messaging, it undermines the implied virality. Personalize each thread to feel genuinely independent.
Med
First clients take too long to close
Near-term revenue pressure is real. If the first 1–3 conversations extend past July 4 without a commitment, Phase 3 scaling decisions become harder to justify. Prioritize speed-to-proposal over perfect qualification.
The Phase 2 logic: You are earning the right to scale. One successful webinar, one or two closed clients, and a validated pipeline view — that's all Phase 2 needs to deliver. The temptation is to over-expand before anything is proven. Resist it. Deep wins with the first two relationships are worth more than shallow contact with twenty.

Scale the Engine

01
David / Jacob
Expand to 2–3 additional PE groups using the Main Street playbook
What worked with Main Street Capital is now a repeatable pattern. The same webinar-plus-multi-thread model gets applied to the next wave of PE groups. These should not be random — they should be deliberately selected based on portfolio fit. The ideal next targets are PE firms or family offices whose portfolio companies have the clearest MiM-ICP overlap: manufacturing, distribution, or services businesses in the $25M–$100M revenue range.
Selection criteria for Phase 3 targets: Portfolio company revenue band matches MiM ICP. PE firm has an active value creation mandate (not just financial engineering). At least one existing warm intro path exists — direct relationship, shared coach connection, or board-level introduction from a current client. Cold outreach to PE firms with zero warm connection is a Phase 4 paid-acquisition problem, not a Phase 3 relationship problem.
🔄
02
Jacob
Establish one high-value engagement per month per active PE relationship
This is the minimum cadence that keeps PE principals engaged without burning the relationship. The content must be PE-specific, operationally relevant, and genuinely useful — not marketing dressed up as insight. One event, one piece of content, or one meaningful conversation per month per relationship. At five active PE relationships in Phase 3, that is five touchpoints per month minimum across the whole team.
What counts as a high-value engagement: A webinar or exclusive event. A PE-specific insight piece (not generic MiM content). A direct introduction to a portco CEO who is ready to talk. A one-on-one strategy session with the PE operating partner. What does not count: a newsletter blast, a social media tag, or a generic check-in call with no new value delivered.
🌐
03
All
Activate the family office → portco intro pipeline at full scale
This is the most powerful lever in the entire acquisition model and the one most likely to be underleveraged. HCPI, Valador, Borgman, and ACVI are not just clients or partners — they are distribution nodes. Each family office principal has board-level authority to direct their portfolio companies to vendors. One conversation with the right FO principal can generate three to eight warm portco introductions in a single meeting. Phase 3 is when this lever gets pulled hard and consistently.
Target rate: 2+ portco introductions per active FO relationship per quarter. That means with four active FO relationships — HCPI, Valador, Borgman, ACVI — the theoretical Phase 3 intro pipeline is 8+ warm portco conversations from relationships already in the network. The math to eight total clients is already present in the existing relationship base. The work is activation, not acquisition.
📈
04
David / Jacob
Define CAC and LTV baselines from the first cohort of PE clients
By the time Phase 3 is complete, MiM will have run at least one webinar and closed 1–3 clients. The data from that cohort needs to be formalized into actual numbers: what did it cost — in time, in direct spend, in team hours — to acquire each PE-sourced client? What is the average initial contract value? What is the realistic LTV projection through the portco multiplier? These numbers are what unlock the paid spend decision in Phase 4.
Why this matters: Without CAC/LTV baselines from real PE clients, paid amplification in Phase 4 is speculation. With them, it becomes capital allocation. The difference between those two framings is the difference between a business case that gets approved and one that doesn't. David and Jacob own the calculation; Eric and the broader team provide the time-tracking inputs.

Eric / David

By Phase 3, the team will have real data on which of the three gateway products — Quick Start™, Trade Show Program, or First Impression Suite — is generating the most traction with PE and portco audiences. Double down on the winner. If Quick Start™ is closing fastest, invest in PE-specific Quick Start™ collateral, pricing refinement, and delivery efficiency. If the First Impression Suite is opening doors for pre-transaction portcos, build a PE-specific version of the pitch and the delivery model.
Signals to watch: Which program generates the most inbound inquiries from webinar attendees? Which has the fastest time-to-close with PE-sourced leads? Which generates the most natural upsell path into First 180™? The answers will be visible in the RDOS pipeline by mid-Phase 3 if tagging discipline from Phase 2 was maintained.

Eric
Build the PE content engine — webinar series cadence established
By Phase 3, the webinar model is no longer a one-off event — it is a recurring engine. At least one PE-exclusive webinar per month. Each event targets a specific PE pain point: AI for portfolio operations, growth systems for founder-led portcos, trade show ROI for industrial portfolio companies, generational transition management. Eric owns the content calendar, the production, and the distribution stack from each event.
Distribution stack per event: Live event (exclusive PE/FO invite list). Long-form blog post published within 5 days. Three LinkedIn short clips within 7 days. Email sequence to all attendees — four touches over 30 days. One-page PDF leave-behind for hot leads. The goal is that any PE principal who attended a MiM event in Phase 2 receives a new piece of PE-relevant content at least every two weeks through Phase 3 without the team writing anything net-new.
The multiplier math — why 3 firms = 8 clients
3 active PE/FO relationships
If each of three PE or family office relationships yields a conservative average of 3 portco introductions, that is 9 potential portco conversations — more than the 8 clients needed to hit $2M. The entire acquisition target is achievable through the multiplier from existing relationship depth alone.
First 180™ upsell window opens
Phase 3 is when the first Quick Start™ clients who closed in Phase 2 begin their workshop sequence. The natural upsell window into First 180™ — at $5K–$15K per month — opens approximately 6 weeks into the Quick Start™ engagement. David owns these upsell conversations.
Broader GTM must stay alive
PE cannot be the only channel. The team aligned on May 20 that over-indexing on one channel creates fragility. PE learnings — the hooks that work, the language that resonates, the objections that surface — must feed back into the broader MiM GTM engine throughout Phase 3.
Risks to manage in Phase 3
High
FO intro pipeline underactivated
The family office lever is the highest-value play in the model. If HCPI, Valador, Borgman, or ACVI are not being actively worked in Phase 3, the multiplier math breaks down and reaching 8 clients requires significantly more cold outreach work.
Med
CAC data not captured cleanly
If the team doesn't log time and direct costs against PE acquisition in Phase 2 and Phase 3, the CAC calculation in late Phase 3 will be a guess — and a guess does not unlock a paid spend budget in Phase 4.
Med
Gateway program selection unclear
If the team tries to sell all three gateway products equally in Phase 3 instead of doubling down on the one with the most PE traction, energy gets divided and close rates stay flat. Pick a lead product by mid-Phase 3.
The Phase 3 logic: The engine is now running. Phase 3 is about turning what worked in Phase 2 into a machine — a monthly cadence, a content stack, a portco intro pipeline, and a data model that justifies paid spend. The work is operational, not experimental. The relationships that produce the most portco introductions get the most attention. Everything else is secondary.

By Phase 4, MiM has a cohort of PE principals and portco CEOs who are active engaged clients. All

The PE channel cannot end 2026 as an isolated success. Everything learned in six months of PE GTM — the messaging that resonated, the content formats that converted, the objections that were hardest to overcome, the personas that engaged most readily — must be codified and fed into the broader MiM market approach. The /insights/private-equity/ content cluster on the website, the PE solutions page at /solutions/for-private-equity, and the broader content calendar all need to reflect what the team actually learned from live market contact, not just what they theorized in planning sessions.

By November 20, the PE learnings should be making the trade show channel better, the cold outreach channel better, and the inbound content channel better. The value of six months in PE is not just the PE clients — it is how much sharper the entire growth motion becomes because of what the PE motion taught the team about how MiM's best customers actually think.
